Lemon
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Lemons are essential in mixology for their vibrant citrus flavor and versatile use in enhancing the aroma and taste of drinks. Beyond their juice, lemons are valued for their aromatic zest and bright peel, which can be fashioned into twists or slices to garnish cocktails and add visual appeal.
The zest, derived from the outer peel, contains flavorful oils that contribute a fresh and zesty essence when twisted over a drink or rubbed along the rim of a glass. Lemon slices, often used as a decorative garnish, not only enhance the presentation of beverages but also subtly infuse them with a hint of citrus flavor. Their acidic juice serves as a foundational ingredient in many cocktails, balancing sweetness and adding a refreshing tang that complements a wide range of spirits and mixers.
This versatility makes lemons a staple in mixology, prized for their ability to elevate the flavor profile and overall drinking experience of cocktails in both simple and sophisticated ways.

Lemon Oil
In The French Martini, the Lemon which is used is specified as Lemon Oil, squeezed from a Lemon Peel.
Lemon Slice
In The Bramble, The Long Island Iced Tea, and The Russian Spring Punch, the Lemon which is used is specified as Lemon Slice.
Lemon Twist
In The Americano and The Dry Martini, the Lemon which is used is specified as Lemon Twist.
Lemon Wedge
In The Bloody Mary and The Trinidad Sour, the Lemon which is used is specified as Lemon Wedge.
Lemon Zest
In The Bee's Knees, The Horse's Neck, The Martinez, The New York Sour, The Sazerac, The Tuxedo, The Ve.n.to, and The Vesper, the Lemon which is used is specified as Lemon Zest.
