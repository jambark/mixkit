Rye
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-50% (80-100 Proof)

Rye whiskey is a distinct style of American whiskey known for its bold and spicy flavor profile, derived from a mash bill that includes at least 51% rye grain, along with corn, malted barley, and sometimes other grains. This composition contrasts with other types of whiskey such as bourbon, which primarily uses corn, and malt whiskey, which predominantly uses malted barley.
Rye whiskey is characterized by its robust taste, often featuring notes of pepper, cinnamon, and sometimes fruity nuances, imparted during the aging process in new charred oak barrels. Its spicy nature makes rye whiskey a favored choice for cocktails like the Manhattan, where its assertive flavor stands out even when mixed with vermouth and bitters.
Rye whiskey's resurgence in popularity reflects a growing appreciation for its distinctive taste and historical significance in American whiskey tradition.
Rye is used in:
  • The Manhattan
  • The Vieux Carre
  • The Trinidad Sour
