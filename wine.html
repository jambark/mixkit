Wine
Type: Liquor
ABV: 9-16% (18-32 Proof)

Wine is a widely enjoyed alcoholic beverage made from fermented grapes or other fruits like berries. It has a rich cultural and historical significance across various regions of the world, where different grape varieties and winemaking techniques produce a vast array of styles and flavors.
Wines can be broadly categorized into red, white, and rosé, each offering distinct characteristics based on grape type, terroir (environmental factors affecting grape growth), and aging processes.
Red wines derive their color and tannins from grape skins during fermentation, resulting in bold flavors often associated with black fruits and spices. White wines are typically lighter in color and can range from crisp and citrusy to rich and creamy, depending on grape variety and vinification methods. Rosé wines, made from red grape varieties with limited skin contact, exhibit a spectrum of hues and flavors that balance between red and white wine characteristics. Whether enjoyed as a complement to meals, for celebrations, or as a standalone pleasure, wine continues to captivate enthusiasts worldwide for its diversity and nuanced flavors.
