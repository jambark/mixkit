Vermouth
Type: Liquor
ABV: 15-18% (30-36 Proof)

Vermouth is a fortified wine infused with a variety of botanicals, herbs, and spices, often including ingredients like wormwood, cinnamon, citrus peel, and cloves. Originating in Europe, particularly Italy and France, vermouth is distinguished by its distinct herbal and sometimes slightly sweet flavor profile.
It is available in two main styles: sweet (red) vermouth and dry (white) vermouth, each offering unique taste characteristics. Sweet vermouth tends to be richer and more aromatic, while dry vermouth is lighter and often used in cocktails like Martinis and Manhattans.
Vermouth is also enjoyed on its own over ice or as an aperitif before meals due to its stimulating and palate-cleansing properties. Its versatility in both classic and modern cocktails has cemented its status as a staple in mixology and a beloved component of cocktail culture worldwide.
Vermouth is used in:
  • The Americano
  • The Boulevardier
  • The Dry Martini
  • The Hanky Panky
  • The Manhattan
  • The Martinez
  • The Negroni
  • The Tipperary
  • The Tuxedo
  • The Vieux Carre
Dry (White) Vermouth
The drier and ligther version of Vermouth is used in The Dry Martini and The Tuxedo.
Sweet (Red) Vermouth
The sweeter and darker version of Vermouth is used in The Americano, The Boulevardier, The Hanky Panky, The Manhattan, The Martinez, The Negroni, The Tipperary, and The Vieux Carre.
