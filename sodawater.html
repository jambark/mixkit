Soda Water
Type: Mixer
Beverage

Soda water, also referred to as carbonated water or sparkling water, is created by infusing carbon dioxide gas into plain water under pressure, resulting in effervescence and a refreshing fizz.
It is typically unflavored, maintaining a clean and crisp taste that complements various beverages. Soda water varies in carbonation levels, from lightly bubbly to highly effervescent, offering versatility in mixology and as a standalone drink.
Distinct from soda water, club soda is carbonated water that has been enhanced with minerals like sodium bicarbonate and potassium sulfate to impart a slightly salty or mineral-like flavor. These additives are intended to mimic the taste of natural mineral waters and provide a distinct tanginess to club soda.
Both soda water and club soda are popular choices in mixology for their effervescence and ability to elevate the texture and flavor of cocktails and other beverages.
Soda Water is used in:
  • The Americano
  • The Gin Fizz
  • The John Collins
  • The Mojito
  • The Ramos Fizz
  • The Spritz
