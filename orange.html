Orange
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Oranges, citrus fruits renowned for their bright color and tangy-sweet flavor, are cultivated globally in warm climates. They grow on evergreen trees known as citrus sinensis, where they start as green fruits before maturing into the familiar orange hue. Oranges are harvested when fully ripe, typically in winter for sweet varieties and year-round for other types.
Their flavor profile is characterized by a juicy, refreshing sweetness with a hint of acidity, making them versatile in culinary applications. In mixology, oranges are valued for their ability to add vibrant citrus notes that enhance both alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages. Whether used as freshly squeezed juice, zest for aromatics, or garnishes like wedges or twists, oranges bring a lively freshness that complements a wide range of flavors in cocktails, contributing both depth and brightness to the drinking experience.
