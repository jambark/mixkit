Rum
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-50% (80-100 Proof)

Rum, a potent and versatile spirit, holds deep historical significance tied to the Caribbean and American colonial eras. Originating from the sugarcane plantations of the Caribbean islands, rum emerged as a byproduct of sugar production in the 17th century. Its early days were marked by trade and commerce, often involving triangular trade routes between Europe, Africa, and the Americas.
Rum played a pivotal role in the economies of colonial powers like Britain and France, serving as a valuable commodity for trade and naval fleets. Over time, its production techniques diversified across the Caribbean, resulting in a wide range of styles from light and clear rums to dark and aged varieties.
Today, rum remains a beloved spirit globally, appreciated for its diverse flavors influenced by aging in oak barrels, and for its cultural resonance, connecting drinkers to centuries of maritime history and Caribbean traditions.
