Olive
Type: Garnish
Vegetable

Olives are small, oval-shaped fruits harvested from the olive tree (Olea europaea), native to the Mediterranean region but now cultivated worldwide. The fruit starts off green and transitions to various shades of purple and black as it ripens.
Olives are typically harvested in the fall, and their bitterness is reduced through curing processes involving brining, fermenting, or dry-curing methods. This results in their characteristic savory and slightly tangy flavor profile.
In mixology, olives are used primarily for their distinctive briny taste and texture, which can add depth and complexity to cocktails without overpowering other flavors. Whole olives or their brine are employed as garnishes or ingredients in savory cocktails and mocktails, contributing a salty note that balances sweetness and acidity in drinks.
