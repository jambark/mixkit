Mezcal
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-50% (80-100 Proof)

Mezcal is a traditional Mexican spirit distilled from the heart of the agave plant, known as the pi√±a. It shares its origins with tequila, another agave-based spirit, but distinguishes itself through a more artisanal and diverse production process.
Unlike tequila, which is primarily made from the blue agave in specific regions of Mexico, mezcal can be crafted from various agave species, giving rise to a broader range of flavor profiles influenced by the plant's terroir and the traditional methods of production.
One of the defining characteristics of mezcal is its smoky essence, derived from the agave hearts being roasted in underground pits before fermentation and distillation. This ancient technique not only imparts a distinctive flavor but also reflects mezcal's deep cultural roots in Oaxaca and other Mexican states where it is crafted. Appreciated both neat and in cocktails, mezcal's complexity and artisanal appeal have garnered international acclaim, making it a celebrated spirit enjoyed for its rich history and diverse flavor expressions.
