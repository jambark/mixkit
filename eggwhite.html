Egg White
Type: Mixer
Emulsifier

Egg white is a key ingredient in mixology, prized for its ability to add a silky texture and frothy foam to cocktails. To separate an egg white from the yolk, crack a fresh egg over a bowl, then gently transfer the yolk back and forth between the two halves of the eggshell, letting the white fall into the bowl below.
Alternatively, use an egg separator or your clean hands to carefully lift the yolk away from the white, letting the white drip into a separate container.
In mixology, egg whites are often dry-shaken (shaken without ice) first to emulsify and create a foam, then shaken again with ice and other ingredients to chill the cocktail. This process not only adds a creamy texture but also enhances the presentation and mouthfeel of drinks, particularly in classic cocktails like sours and fizzes where the egg white's properties are showcased.
