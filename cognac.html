Cognac
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-43% (80-86 Proof)

Cognac is a type of brandy that originates from the Cognac region in France, renowned worldwide for its distinctive production methods and rich history. It is made from white grapes, primarily Ugni Blanc, which are harvested, pressed into juice, fermented, and then distilled twice in copper pot stills.
The resulting clear spirit is aged in French oak barrels for at least two years, often much longer, allowing it to develop complex flavors and aromas. Cognac is categorized by age labels such as VS (Very Special), VSOP (Very Superior Old Pale), and XO (Extra Old), indicating the minimum aging period.
It is known for its smooth texture, notes of fruit, oak, and spice, and is typically enjoyed as a digestif or used in fine cocktails. Cognac's production is tightly regulated by French law to ensure quality and authenticity, and it remains a symbol of luxury and refinement in the world of spirits.
