Scotch
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-46% (80-92 Proof)

Scotch whiskey, revered for its rich heritage and distinct production methods, originates exclusively from Scotland. It is crafted primarily from malted barley or a blend of grains, then distilled and aged in oak barrels for a minimum of three years.
What sets Scotch apart from other types of whiskey is its adherence to strict regulations that define its production processes, ensuring authenticity and quality. Scotch whisky is further distinguished by its regional variations, including Highlands, Lowlands, Speyside, Islay, and others, each imparting unique flavors influenced by local ingredients and distillation techniques. This diversity results in a spectrum of flavors ranging from peat-smoke and maritime notes in Islay whiskies to the fruity and floral characteristics of Speyside malts.
Scotch whisky holds a prominent place in global spirits culture, prized for its depth of flavor, craftsmanship, and centuries-old tradition upheld by distilleries across Scotland.
