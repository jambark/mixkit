Bourbon
Type: Liquor
ABV: 40-50% (80-100 Proof)

Bourbon is a distinctive type of American whiskey celebrated for its rich history and unique production methods. It must be made from a grain mixture that is predominantly corn, distilled to a certain proof, and aged in new charred oak barrels. This aging process, which typically spans several years, imparts bourbon with flavors of vanilla, caramel, and oak, resulting in a smooth and complex spirit.
Bourbon's strict production standards ensure a consistent level of quality, contributing to its popularity both domestically and internationally. It is enjoyed straight, on the rocks, or as the base for classic cocktails, making it an integral part of American whiskey culture and a favorite among enthusiasts worldwide.
Bourbon is used in:
  • The Boulevardier
  • The Mint Julep
  • The Paper Plane
  • The Whiskey Sour
