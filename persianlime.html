Persian Lime
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Persian limes, also known as Tahiti limes, are primarily cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions such as Mexico, India, and the Middle East. Unlike their smaller, more common counterpart, the Key lime, Persian limes are larger with a thicker peel and a less acidic flavor profile. They are typically harvested when green but turn yellow upon ripening.
Persian limes are favored in mixology for their juicy, tangy-sweet taste that balances acidity and sweetness in drinks without overpowering other flavors. Their larger size also makes them easier to juice, providing a convenient source of fresh lime juice for cocktails and mocktails alike. In addition to being used for their juice, Persian limes' aromatic zest is prized for its citrusy essence, which enhances the aroma and overall flavor complexity of beverages.
