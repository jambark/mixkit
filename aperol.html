Aperol
Type: Liquor
ABV: 11% (22 Proof)

Aperol is a vibrant Italian drink renowned for its distinctive orange hue and bittersweet flavor profile. Created in 1919 by the Barbieri brothers in Padua, Italy, it combines bitter and sweet oranges, rhubarb, gentian, and various herbs and roots to achieve its unique taste.
Aperol is characterized by its refreshing citrus notes and mild bitterness, making it less intense than similar aperitifs. Its most famous application is in the Aperol Spritz, a popular cocktail that mixes Aperol with Prosecco and soda water, garnished with a slice of orange.
Aperol has enjoyed a resurgence in global popularity, particularly during warm months, owing to its versatility in cocktails and its role as a refreshing pre-dinner drink.
Aperol is used in:
  • The Spritz
  • The Naked and Famous
  • The Paper Plane
