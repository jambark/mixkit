Absinthe
Type: Liquor
ABV: 55-72% (110-144 Proof)

Absinthe is a potent, aromatic spirit characterized by its vibrant green hue and complex herbal flavors derived from a blend of botanicals including wormwood, anise, and fennel.
Historically infamous in Europe during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, absinthe was associated with bohemian culture and myths of hallucinogenic effects, leading to its prohibition in many countries.
Traditionally served by dripping ice-cold water over a sugar cube into the spirit, causing it to louche (turn cloudy), absinthe is enjoyed for its ritualistic preparation and its bold, licorice-like taste.
Today, absinthe is produced under strict regulations, appreciated for its unique flavor profile in both traditional serves and modern cocktails, marking its resurgence and enduring allure in the world of spirits.
