Pineapple
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Pineapples are tropical fruits grown primarily in regions with warm climates, such as Central and South America, Southeast Asia, and Hawaii. They grow on a low, herbaceous plant with a cluster of long, spiky leaves and produce a single fruit per plant. Pineapples mature in about 12-18 months after planting, developing a distinctive crown and rough, hexagonal patterned skin that turns from green to yellow as it ripens.
The fruit's flavor profile is characterized by a harmonious blend of sweetness and tartness, with a juicy texture and a subtly acidic tang.
In mixology, pineapples are valued for their sweet and slightly tangy juice, which adds tropical flair and natural sweetness to cocktails and mocktails. Beyond their juice, pineapple chunks or wedges can be used as garnishes to impart a fresh, fruity aroma and enhance the visual appeal of drinks, making them a versatile ingredient in the bartender's toolkit.
Pineapples are used in:
  • The Barracuda
  • The Mai Tai
  • The Piña Colada
  • The Singapore Sling
Pineapple Slice
In The Barracuda, The Piña Colada, and The Singapore Sling, the Pineapple which is used is specified as Pineapple Slice.
Pineapple Spear
In The Mai Tai, the Pineapple which is used is specified as Pineapple Spear.
