Raspberry
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Raspberries are small, delicate berries known for their vibrant red color and sweet-tart flavor. They are cultivated primarily in temperate regions throughout the world and are characterized by their aggregate fruit structure, composed of numerous small drupelets clustered around a central core.
Raspberries boast a juicy and slightly tangy taste, with a pleasant balance of sweetness and acidity that varies depending on their ripeness. In mixology, raspberries are prized for their intense flavor and vibrant color, which add depth and complexity to drinks without overwhelming other ingredients.
They can be used in various forms: muddled for their juicy essence, strained for their bright puree, or simply floated as a garnish to provide a burst of fruity aroma and visual appeal. Raspberries also lend a natural sweetness that complements a wide range of spirits and mixers, making them a versatile and sought-after ingredient in cocktail creation.
