Salt Rim
Type: Garnish
Rim

Salt rims in mixology serve both functional and aesthetic purposes, enhancing the drinking experience in several ways. Typically, coarse salt such as kosher or sea salt is used to rim the glass by moistening the rim with a citrus wedge or water and then dipping it into the salt.
This creates a salty contrast that complements the flavors of certain cocktails, particularly those with citrus or savory profiles, by balancing sweetness and acidity.
Beyond taste, the salt rim also provides a tactile and visual element, adding texture and a touch of sophistication to the presentation of the drink. It serves as a signal to the drinker of the cocktail's flavor profile before the first sip, enhancing anticipation and overall enjoyment. Additionally, the salt rim can influence the perception of the drink's temperature and mouthfeel, subtly altering the drinking experience in a way that complements the cocktail's ingredients and intended flavor profile.
