Aguardiente
Type: Liquor
ABV: 29-60% (58-120 Proof)

Aguardiente is a potent alcoholic beverage widely consumed in Latin America and Spain. It is traditionally made through the fermentation and distillation of sugarcane, though variations using other fruits like grapes or apples exist.
The name "aguardiente," translating to "firewater," reflects its strong and often fiery nature. It holds cultural significance in many countries, often being associated with celebrations and social gatherings.
Aguardiente is enjoyed neat, as a shot, or in cocktails, depending on regional preferences and traditions, and its flavor profiles can range from mildly sweet to intensely herbal, depending on production methods and regional variations.
