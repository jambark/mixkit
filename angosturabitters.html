Angostura Bitters
Type: Liqueur
ABV: 44.7% (89.4 Proof)

Angostura Bitters, originating from Trinidad and Tobago, are renowned for their pivotal role in enhancing the flavor and aroma of cocktails and culinary creations alike.
Created by Johann Siegert in the early 19th century as a tonic for soldiers, these bitters boast a complex blend of botanicals and spices, including gentian, cinnamon, and cloves.
Despite their name suggesting bitterness, they actually add layers of complexity and balance to drinks, often used in small dashes to elevate the overall sensory experience.
Beyond mixology, Angostura Bitters are also prized in cooking, where they lend depth to sauces, marinades, and desserts, showcasing their versatility and enduring appeal across different culinary traditions.
Angostura Bitters are used in:
  • The Champagne Cocktail
  • The Horse's Neck
  • The Manhattan
  • The Old Cuban
  • The Old Fashioned
  • The Singapore Sling
  • The Suffering Bastard
  • The Tipperary
  • The Trinidad Sour
  • The Zombie
