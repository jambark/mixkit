Lime
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Limes are indispensable in mixology for their bright and tangy citrus flavor, which enhances the complexity and freshness of drinks. Beyond their juice, limes are utilized in various forms to elevate cocktails. The aromatic peel of a lime can be fashioned into a zest or twist, releasing flavorful oils that add a fragrant essence when expressed over a drink or used as a garnish.
Lime slices or wedges, often used as decorative additions to beverages, not only enhance visual appeal but also infuse drinks with a refreshing citrus aroma and taste. Their acidic juice is a fundamental ingredient in many cocktails, providing a crisp and tart balance that complements a wide range of spirits and mixers.
Limes are prized for their versatility and ability to add a zesty brightness to cocktails, making them a favored choice among mixologists aiming to craft drinks with vibrant flavors and balanced acidity.
Limes are used in:
  • The Cosmopolitan
  • The Canchanchara
  • The Cuba Libre
  • The Dark 'n' Stormy
  • The Mai Tai
  • The Margarita
  • The Mojito
  • The Paloma
  • The Sea Breeze

Lime Peel
In The Mai Tai, the Lime which is used is specified as Lime Peel.
Lime Slice
In The Cosmopolitan, The Mojito, The Paloma, The Sea Breeze, and The Tommy's Margarita, the Lime which is used is specified as Lime Slice.
Lime Wedge
In The Canchanchara, The Cuba Libre, The Dark 'n' Stormy, and The Margarita, the Lime which is used is specified as Lime Wedge.
