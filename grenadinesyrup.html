Grenadine Syrup
Type: Mixer
Sweetener

Grenadine syrup is a sweet and brightly colored syrup made from pomegranate juice and sugar. Its flavor is characterized by a balance of sweetness with a hint of tartness, reminiscent of its pomegranate origins.
In mixology, grenadine syrup is a versatile ingredient used primarily as a sweetener and coloring agent in cocktails. It adds a vibrant red hue and a subtle fruity undertone to drinks, enhancing their visual appeal while providing a touch of sweetness that balances the flavors of other ingredients.
Bartenders often incorporate grenadine syrup into a variety of cocktails, from tropical drinks to classic favorites, where its distinct flavor and color contribute to both the taste and presentation of the beverage.

  • The Monkey Gland
