Nutmeg
Type: Garnish
Spice

Nutmeg is derived from the seed of the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans) and is harvested from the fruit of the tree, which resembles a small apricot. Once ripe, the fruit splits open, revealing the bright red mace surrounding the brown nutmeg seed. The seed is then dried and ground into a fine powder or used whole.
Nutmeg's flavor profile is warm, aromatic, and slightly sweet, with hints of nuttiness and a subtle spiciness. In mixology, nutmeg is valued for its ability to add depth and complexity to drinks without overwhelming other flavors. Its finely grated powder or freshly grated nut can be used as a garnish to impart a rich aroma and a warm, lingering taste that enhances the overall drinking experience.
Nutmeg is used in:
  • The Alexander
  • The Porto Flip
Grated Nutmeg
In The Porto Flip, the Nutmeg which is used is specified as Grated Nutmeg.
Ground Nutmeg
In The Alexander, the Nutmeg which is used is specified as Ground Nutmeg.
