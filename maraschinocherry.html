Maraschino Cherry
Type: Garnish
Fruit

Maraschino cherries, distinct in appearance and flavor, undergo a unique preservation process that sets them apart from their fresh counterparts. Unlike fresh cherries, which are typically deep red or purple, Maraschinos are bright red, often with a slightly tart flavor due to the brining and sugaring process they undergo.
This preservation method also gives them a firmer texture compared to fresh cherries, making them suitable for garnishing cocktails and desserts without compromising their shape or juiciness. In mixology, their sweetness and vibrant color add visual appeal and a balanced fruity note to drinks, enhancing both appearance and taste.
While they lack the juiciness and nuanced flavor of fresh cherries, Maraschinos are prized for their durability and ability to maintain their form over time, making them a staple in bars and kitchens alike for adding a finishing touch to beverages and sweets.
