Mint
Type: Garnish
Herb

Mint is a versatile herb in mixology, prized for its refreshing and aromatic qualities. Its bright, slightly sweet flavor profile with hints of coolness makes it a perfect complement to a wide range of drinks.
Mint's fresh leaves are often muddled or used as a garnish to release their essential oils, which impart a crisp, herbaceous aroma and a subtle yet distinct taste that can uplift various beverages. Whether used in cocktails, mocktails, or even non-alcoholic refreshers, mint adds a refreshing twist that harmonizes with citrusy, fruity, and herbal flavor profiles.
Its ability to enhance and balance flavors without overpowering them makes it a staple ingredient in mixology, offering a cooling sensation that is particularly welcome during warmer seasons. Beyond its flavor, mint also contributes to the visual appeal of drinks, often used as a garnish to lend a vibrant green hue and a touch of elegance to the presentation.
Mint is used in:
  • The Barracuda
  • The Mai Tai
  • The Mint Julep
  • The Mojito
  • The Old Cuban
  • The Stinger
  • The Suffering Bastard
  • The Zombie
Mint Leaves
In The Mai Tai and The Stinger, the Mint which is used is specified as Mint Leaves. It is also used as a mixer in The Old Cuban.
Mint Sprig
In The Barracuda, The Mint Julep, The Mojito, The Old Cuban, The Suffering Bastard, and The Zombie, the Mint which is used is specified as Mint Sprig. It is also used as a mixer in The Mint Julep and The Mojito.
