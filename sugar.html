Sugar
Type: Mixer
Sweetener

Sugar, a widely used sweetener, is a carbohydrate derived primarily from sugar cane or sugar beet. It comes in various forms such as granulated, powdered, and liquid, each offering unique textures and applications in culinary arts.
Known for its ability to enhance flavors and add sweetness, sugar plays a crucial role in balancing tastes across a spectrum of dishes, from savory sauces to decadent desserts.
In mixology, sugar is essential for sweetening cocktails without altering their flavors significantly. Beyond its role in taste enhancement, sugar contributes to texture and mouthfeel, providing a pleasing sensation that rounds out the drinking experience. Its versatility and culinary significance make sugar a cornerstone in both professional kitchens and home cooking, offering endless possibilities for creating delectable treats and beverages.
Sugar is used in:
  • The Caipirinha
  • The Champagne Cocktail
  • The Daiquiri
  • The Irish Coffee
  • The Mint Julep
  • The Mojito
  • The Old Fashioned
  • The Sazerac
Powdered Sugar
In The Mint Julep, the Sugar which is used is specified as Powdered Sugar.
Sugar Cube
In The Champagne Cocktail, The Old Fashioned, and The Sazerac, the Sugar which is used is specified as Sugar Cube.
Superfine Sugar
In The Daiquiri, the Sugar which is used is specified as Superfine Sugar.
White Cane Sugar
In The Caipirinha and The Mojito, the Sugar which is used is specified as White Cane Sugar.
