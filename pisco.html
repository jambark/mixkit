Pisco
Type: Liquor
ABV: 38-48% (76-96 Proof)

Pisco is a distinctive grape brandy originating from Peru and Chile, where it holds deep cultural and historical significance. Unlike traditional varieties of brandy, which are often aged in wood barrels, pisco is typically bottled shortly after distillation to preserve its fresh and fruity flavors. Produced from specific grape varieties in designated regions, pisco reflects the terroir of its origin, showcasing nuances ranging from floral and citrusy to more robust and aromatic profiles.
Peruvian pisco, distilled to proof, and Chilean pisco, which may be slightly diluted, both adhere to strict production regulations that govern grape varieties, distillation methods, and alcohol content.
The clear spirit is celebrated for its versatility, enjoyed neat as a sipping spirit or in iconic cocktails like the Pisco Sour, where its crisp character and aromatic complexity shine through, making pisco a cherished part of South American culinary and cocktail culture.
