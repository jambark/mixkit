Honey Mix
Type: Mixer
Sweetener

Honey mix differs from straight honey in mixology by being a diluted solution of honey and water, usually in equal parts. This preparation ensures that the honey blends smoothly into cocktails without clumping or settling.
It maintains the natural sweetness and flavor of honey while providing a more fluid consistency that integrates well with other ingredients.
In contrast, honey syrup is a more concentrated version, typically made with a higher ratio of honey to water, which can impart a stronger honey flavor to cocktails.
Bartenders often choose between honey mix and honey syrup based on the desired level of sweetness and how easily they want the honey to mix into the drink. Both options serve to add complexity and depth to cocktails, enhancing their flavor profiles with a touch of natural sweetness from honey.
