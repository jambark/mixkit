Chili Pepper
Type: Garnish
Vegetable

Chili peppers bring a bold and spicy flavor profile to mixology, adding heat and complexity to drinks. Their distinctive taste varies widely depending on the type of chili, ranging from mild and fruity to intensely spicy and smoky. Common varieties used in cocktails include jalape√±o, habanero, and serrano peppers, each offering its unique heat level and flavor nuances.
In mixology, chili peppers are often muddled, infused into spirits or syrups, or used as a garnish to impart their spicy kick and aromatic qualities. They complement a variety of spirits, from clear options like vodka and tequila to darker spirits such as whiskey or rum, allowing mixologists to create drinks that balance heat with other flavor profiles.
Beyond their use in beverages, chili peppers are valued for their versatility in enhancing the overall sensory experience of cocktails, appealing to adventurous palates seeking a bold and invigorating flavor sensation.
