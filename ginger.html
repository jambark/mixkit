Ginger
Type: Garnish
Rhizome

Ginger is a versatile rhizome prized for its spicy and aromatic flavor, widely used in mixology to add depth and complexity to drinks. Classified as a rhizome rather than a root, ginger grows underground and produces shoots and roots along its nodes.
Its pungent taste comes from bioactive compounds such as gingerol and zingerone, which lend a warm and peppery flavor profile. In mixology, ginger is utilized in various forms, including fresh slices, muddled pieces, or ginger-infused syrups, to imbue beverages with its distinct spicy kick and subtle sweetness.
Its ability to harmonize with a wide range of spirits and ingredients makes ginger a favored choice among mixologists seeking to enhance the flavor profile of their creations with a hint of aromatic heat.
