Celery
Type: Garnish
Vegetable

Celery is valued in mixology for its distinctive flavor profile, which is characterized by a crisp and clean taste with a hint of herbal freshness. Often described as earthy and slightly savory, celery adds a unique depth to drinks, enhancing their complexity without overwhelming other ingredients.
Celery is commonly used in mixology in various forms: as a muddled ingredient to release its juices and aromatics, as a garnish to impart its subtle flavor to the drink, or even as an infusion in syrups or bitters. Its versatility allows mixologists to experiment with different flavor combinations, balancing celery's natural vegetal notes with a range of spirits and other ingredients to create nuanced and refreshing cocktails.
Beyond its role in beverages, celery's aromatic qualities and delicate flavor make it a prized ingredient in culinary applications, further highlighting its appeal in both the bar and kitchen.
