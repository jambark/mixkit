Peychaud's Bitters
Type: Liqueur
ABV: 35% (70 Proof)

Peychaud's Bitters, originating from New Orleans in the early 19th century, are renowned for their integral role in classic cocktails such as the Sazerac.
Created by Antoine Amédée Peychaud, a pharmacist of French Creole descent, these bitters are distinguished by their vibrant red hue and complex flavor profile. Infused with aromatic botanicals, they impart subtle notes of anise, mint, and floral hints, adding a distinctive touch to drinks without overpowering their flavors.
Peychaud's Bitters are cherished for their ability to enhance the depth and balance of cocktails, making them a staple ingredient in the repertoire of mixologists and enthusiasts alike, preserving a flavorful legacy rooted in New Orleans' cocktail culture.
Peychaud's Bitters are used in:
  • The Sazerac
  • The Vieux Carre
